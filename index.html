Marvel Icons

Circular icons of some popular Marvel characters. Current characters include: Daredevil, Deadpool, Dr. Doom, Dr. Strange, Iron Fist, Iron Man, The Punisher, Spider-Man, Thor, and Wolverine. Click on any icon for more information.

Daredevil

Daredevil, also known as "Man Without Fear" and the "Devil of Hell's Kitchen", was created by writer-editor Stan Lee and artist Bill Everett and first appeared in Daredevil #1 (April 1964). His abilities include Superhuman senses, echolocative radar sense, skilled acrobat and hand-to-hand combatant, and utilization of a specially-designed club.

Deadpool

Deadpool was created by writer Fabian Nicieza and artist/writer Rob Liefeld and first appeared in The New Mutants #98 (February 1991). His abilities include a regenerative healing factor, extended longevity, skilled marksmanship, swordsmanship, and hand-to-hand combat, and fourth wall awareness. He also uses a magic satchel and owns devices granting teleportation and holographic disguise.

Dr. Doom

Dr. Doom was created by writer-editor Stan Lee and artist/co-plotter Jack Kirby and first appeared in The Fantastic Four #5 (July 1962). His abilities include genius-level intellect, technopathy, energy absorption and projection, mind transference, and demonic summoning via dark mysticism and sorcery. His armor grants him superhuman strength and durability, gauntlet lasers and force blasts, flight via rocket boots, forcefield generation, and various high-tech weapons and gadgets

Dr. Strange

Dr. Strange was created by artist Steve Ditko and writer Stan Lee and first appeared in Strange Tales #110 (July 1963). His abilities include mastery of magic, genius-level intelect, martial arts, and is a trained physician. He also utilizes mystical artifacts, such as the cloak of Levitation and the Eye of Agamotto.

Iron Man

Iron Man was co-created by writer and editor Stan Lee, developed by scripter Larry Lieber, and designed by artists Don Heck and Jack Kirby and first appeared in Tales of Suspense #39 (March 1963). His abilities include genius-level intellect, and proficiency in science and engineering. His powered suits grant him superhuman strength and durability, supersonic flight, energy repulsors and missile projection, and regenerative life support.

The Punisher

The Punisher was created by writer Gerry Conway and artists John Romita Sr. and Ross Andru, with publisher Stan Lee green-lighting the name and first appeared in The Amazing Spider-Man #129 (February 1974). His abilities include proficiency in guerrilla and military tactics, close quarters combat, infiltration, marksmanship, and demolitions and special operations training.

Spider-Man

Spider-Man was created by writer-editor Stan Lee and writer-artist Steve Ditko and first appeared Amazing Fantasy #15 (August 1962). His abilities include genius-level intellect, proficiency in science and inventing, superhuman strength, speed, agility, stamina, reflexes, coordination, balance, and endurance, precognitive spider-sense, and the ability to cling to most solid surfaces. He also utilizes wrist web-shooters to shoot spiderweb material.

Thor

Thor was created by editor-plotter Stan Lee, scripter Larry Lieber, and penciller-plotter Jack Kirby and first appeared in Journey into Mystery #83 (August 1962). His abilities include superhuman strength, speed, durability and longevity. He also wields a mystical hammer called Mjolnir which grants him dimensional transportation, electric manipulation, flight, and weather manipulation.

Wolverine

Wolverine was created by Marvel editor-in-chief Roy Thomas, writer Len Wein, and Marvel art director John Romita Sr. Romita and first appeared in The Incredible Hulk #180 (October 1974). His abilities include superhuman senses, agility, reflexes, and animal-like attributes, extended longevity via regenerative healing factor, an adamantium-plated skeleton, retractable bone claws, and skills in hand-to-hand combat.
